Dynamics of SSL Audio Models

Investigating layer-wise information compression, geometric manifolds, and discriminative invariance.

Abstract

"Self-supervised learning models like Wav2Vec2, HuBERT, and WavLM learn rich speech representations, yet their internal layer-wise dynamics remain unclear. We analyze these models through three perspectives: entropy, curvature, and invariance. Our study reveals patterns of compression and late-layer sensitivity shifts."

Quick Summary

  • 1. Models increase complexity before unfolding manifolds for separability.
  • 2. Scaling size (Large) shifts geometric organization more than data volume.

Training Dynamics &
Geometric Relaxation

Evolution of embedding curvature during the first training iteration of a HuBERT model, from initialization to convergence.

Rapid Encoding

Initially, the model exhibits low and uniform curvature (≈ 1.1). Optimization triggers a sharp surge (≈ 1.4), reflecting the rapid encoding of acoustic complexity.

Structural Separation

As training progresses, a separation emerges: early layers (0–40%) maintain high curvature for intricate features, while deep layers (> 40%) undergo progressive relaxation.

Linearization Phase

In the final phase, curvature drops in deeper layers, signaling a linearization process where the model "flattens" its representations to facilitate linear separability.

1. Phonetic Consistency
